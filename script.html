About Us

K Productions stands as a professional and innovative production company, founded by in 2018. The organisation adheres to the terms and policies outlined by NPO regulations in South Africa. Our focus revolves around pioneering concepts that have an impact on our nation's development, approached with a fresh perspective. Although the company is relatively new, it has garnered significant support and partnerships from esteemed entities such as the Department of Education, Matthew Goniwe School of Leadership and Governance, University of Pretoria, BASA, ATKV, NETCB, City of Tshwane, Sun International, Music is an Investment, The Department of International Relations (DIRCO), European Union and other pertinent bodies. Through these alliances, the production has gained access to collaborate with numerous underperforming schools in and out of South Africa, a testament to the rapport we have built.

Our original works have garnered international recognition and support, notably in South America, specifically Argentina - Bueno Aires, La Plata, and Greece - Kefalonia Athens, facilitated by the Department of Arts and Culture. Our productions have resonated with over 25,000 individuals, including learners and members of the community. This reach has been achieved by partnering with 21 secondary schools in recent years in conjunction with the Department of Education.

K Productions remains dedicated to reigniting a passion for performing arts through our subsequent productions. Theatre serves as our medium of communication, a powerful vehicle for education and inspiration. Our team of key members and facilitators are well-qualified and experienced in delivering these services.

Gender-Based Violence (GBV) refers to any harmful act directed at an individual based on their gender, resulting in physical, sexual, psychological, or economic harm. Fighting GBV is essential as it violates basic human rights, undermines social progress, and perpetuates inequalities. It is a pressing global issue that requires collective efforts to eliminate.

Prevalence of GBV in South Africa: Statistics reveal the urgent need to address GBV in South Africa. According to a study by Statistics South Africa (Stats SA), the country experiences high levels of GBV. In 2020, the police recorded 58,344 sexual offenses, and there were 2,695 femicides reported. These alarming numbers underscore the severity of the problem and emphasize the necessity of proactive interventions.

K productions is an activist in the fight against GBV. It uses various art forms to highlight some of the subtleties that have become societal norms, which give birth to GBV. Art forms as expressive vehicles allow the audience introspective space using humour as a gateway.

Human rights are fundamental principles that acknowledge the inherent value and dignity of every individual.
